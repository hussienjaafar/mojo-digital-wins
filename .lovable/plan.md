

# Deep Conversion Audit: /get-started Landing Page

## Executive Summary

The current page is a solid foundation but has **7 critical conversion gaps** based on research from high-performing paid social funnels, B2B landing page anatomy studies, and Meta ad optimization best practices. Fixing these could realistically lift conversion rates by 40-80% based on documented case studies with similar changes.

---

## AUDIT FINDINGS

### 1. MISSING: Problem Agitation (Critical Gap)

**Current state:** The page jumps straight from "Audience Intelligence Platform" headline to features and how-it-works. There is zero problem framing.

**Research says:** The highest-converting B2B pages include a "Problem Clarity" section immediately after the hero. When visitors see their problem described in precise words, trust increases dramatically. Pages that agitate the pain before presenting the solution convert 30-45% better (Monad MarTech A/B testing data).

**What's missing:** A section like "You're spending on ads but can't verify you're reaching the right people" or "Most campaigns waste 40%+ of media spend on the wrong audience" -- something that makes the visitor feel understood before being sold to.

**Recommendation:** Add a 2-3 line "Problem" section between LogoBar and StatsSection. Frame the pain specifically for each audience (political: wasted donor dollars; commercial: low ROAS).

---

### 2. HERO: Headline Lacks Specificity and Outcome Clarity

**Current state:** "Reach the Right Audience. Every Channel." -- This is a feature statement, not an outcome promise.

**Research says:** Headlines that state a specific, measurable outcome outperform vague benefit statements. The formula is: **State the outcome plainly, then clarify the audience and the problem you solve** (Monad MarTech). MNTN's competitor page leads with a specific value prop: "Performance TV for the people."

**Issues:**
- "Audience Intelligence Platform" badge means nothing to cold traffic who just clicked an ad
- The subheadline lists channels (CTV, digital, etc.) which is internal jargon to someone who just saw a Meta ad
- No specificity about WHO this is for or WHAT outcome they get

**Recommendation:** Rewrite to be outcome-first. Examples:
- "Stop Wasting Ad Spend on the Wrong Audience"
- "Know Exactly Who to Target -- Before You Spend a Dollar"
- The badge should say something like "Used by 50+ campaigns" (social proof) instead of a category label

---

### 3. MISSING: Social Proof Above the Fold (Critical for Paid Social)

**Current state:** The hero has zero social proof. No customer count, no metric, no logo. The logo bar is below the fold.

**Research says:** "Immediate social proof -- customer count, review scores, or recognizable logos -- above the fold" is one of the top trust velocity signals. A study showed that adding "Join 12,847 remote teams" instead of generic copy increased trial sign-ups by 156% (Jankovic). For paid social specifically, 78% of traffic is mobile -- they may never scroll past the hero.

**Recommendation:** Add a small social proof line directly in the hero section, like "Trusted by 50+ campaigns and organizations" or "Helped raise $2.7M+ across 13,500 new donors" right above or below the CTA button.

---

### 4. CTA: Button Copy Lacks Urgency and Value Specificity

**Current state:** "Get Your Free Report" (hero) and "Start Your Free Report" (bottom). Both are decent but generic.

**Research says:** CTA buttons that include a time element or specificity convert better. "Get Your Free Report in 2 Minutes" outperforms "Get Your Free Report." The trust badge says "Takes 2 minutes" but that should be IN the button or subtext directly adjacent.

**Issues:**
- Two different CTA phrasings ("Get Your" vs "Start Your") creates inconsistency
- No urgency or scarcity element anywhere on the page
- The nav CTA says "Get Started" which is a third variant -- confusion

**Recommendation:**
- Unify all CTAs to one phrase: "Get My Free Report" (first person = higher conversion)
- Add time specificity: "Get My Free Report -- Takes 2 Minutes"
- Nav CTA should match: "Get Free Report" instead of "Get Started"

---

### 5. STATS: Numbers Lack Context and Credibility Framing

**Current state:** "$2.7M+ Raised", "13,500+ New Donors", "5+ Channels", "947% Peak ROI" -- displayed as flat cards.

**Issues:**
- These stats are heavily political-leaning ("Raised", "Donors"). A commercial prospect seeing this immediately feels "this isn't for me"
- "5+ Channels" is not impressive as a stat -- it's a feature
- No source attribution. "947% ROI" without context looks like a made-up number
- Missing a stat that matters to commercial: something about match rates, audience accuracy, or media savings

**Recommendation:**
- Replace "5+ Channels" with something like "92% Average Match Rate" or "50+ Organizations Served"
- Add micro-attribution: "947% ROI -- Unity and Justice Fund" (already in the data but not shown in the stat card)
- Balance commercial and political proof. Add a commercial-relevant metric

---

### 6. MISSING: Risk Reversal is Weak

**Current state:** "No commitment required. Takes 2 minutes" (hero) and "No credit card. No commitment. 2-minute setup" (bottom CTA). These are good but buried.

**Research says:** Risk reversal is one of the most underused conversion elements. "Lower the perceived risk and more users will convert." Examples: free strategy session, no-obligation consultation, short form that does not ask for excessive data, privacy reassurance (Monad MarTech). The Jankovic research shows risk elimination should be prominent, not an afterthought.

**Recommendation:**
- Make risk reversal more prominent in the hero -- potentially as a small checklist (checkmark icons) rather than a single line of muted text
- Add "Your data stays private" or similar privacy reassurance since you're in the data/audience targeting space

---

### 7. PAGE STRUCTURE: Missing Mid-Page CTA Breaks

**Current state:** There are only 2 CTA buttons -- hero and final section. Between them are 5 content sections with zero conversion opportunities.

**Research says:** "A single primary CTA across the page" means the SAME CTA repeated, not just one instance. Best practice is to include the CTA after every 2-3 sections so visitors can convert whenever they're convinced -- not just at the very top and very bottom. Reducing the scroll distance to conversion improves rates by 15-22% (Monad MarTech).

**Recommendation:** Add a mid-page CTA after the "How It Works" section and optionally after "Channel Showcase." These don't need to be massive -- a simple inline "Get My Free Report" button is sufficient.

---

## SECONDARY ISSUES

### 8. Mobile: CTA Button Not in Thumb Zone
The mobile screenshot shows the CTA in the center of the screen. Research indicates primary CTAs should be in the bottom third of the screen for thumb reachability. Consider a sticky mobile CTA bar.

### 9. No Video or Visual Demonstration
MNTN and other high-converting competitors use product screenshots, demo videos, or animated previews. The page is 100% text and icons. Even a simple mockup of what the "opportunity report" looks like would significantly boost understanding and desire.

### 10. Testimonials Lack Photos
Research consistently shows that testimonials with photos convert 35% better than text-only. Currently showing name and title only.

### 11. No FAQ or Objection Handling
There is no section addressing common objections: "How is this different from my current data provider?", "What data sources do you use?", "Is this compliant?"
